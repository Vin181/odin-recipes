Takoyaki

What is Takoyaki?

Takoyaki is a popular Japanese snack consisting of ball-shaped batter filled with diced octopus, cooked in a special molded pan, and served with various sauces and toppings.

Quick Info

  • Prep time: 15 mins
  • Cook time: 20-25 mins
  • Total time: 35-40 mins
  • Yield: Makes approximately 24 takoyaki balls

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 2 cups dashi (Japanese soup stock)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup cooked octopus, diced into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup green onions, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup pickled red ginger, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup tempura scraps (tenkasu)
  • 1/4 cup dried shrimp (ebi), finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup beni shoga (pickled red ginger)
  • Takoyaki sauce
  • Mayonnaise
  • Bonito flakes
  • Aonori (dried green seaweed flakes)

How to Cook

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Slowly add the dashi and eggs to the dry ingredients, stirring constantly until you have a smooth batter.
  3. Add the cooked octopus, green onions, pickled red ginger, tempura scraps, dried shrimp, and beni shoga to the batter, and mix until everything is evenly distributed.
  4. Preheat your takoyaki pan over medium heat. Grease the pan with cooking spray or a small amount of oil.
  5. Use a ladle to pour batter into each mold of the takoyaki pan, filling them to just below the rim.
  6. Use chopsticks or a skewer to flip each takoyaki ball over as soon as the edges start to cook and become firm. Continue to turn the balls so that they cook evenly on all sides.
  7. Once the takoyaki balls are golden brown on all sides, remove them from the pan and place them on a plate.
  8. Drizzle takoyaki sauce and mayonnaise over the top of the takoyaki balls.
  9. Sprinkle bonito flakes and aonori over the top of the takoyaki balls. Serve while hot.

Enjoy your hot and crispy takoyaki balls perfect as snack or appetizer!
